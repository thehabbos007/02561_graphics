Projection shadows are easy to implement, as it is possible to do so within the same WebGL program, but the cons of using this method is visible from the results. The shadow is somewhat flickery and can error prone when it comes to culling. Shadow mapping, while more complicated, yield better results. The shadow doesn't flicker, and the depth packing technique allows for shadow edges on par with projection shadows. Shadow mapping would also allow to have self-shadowing.
